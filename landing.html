Introducing Daniel Ko
Who I am, What I do, What I'm like

Introduction

Hi, my name is Daniel Ko, and I'm currently a senior in high school from Southern California. I'm passionate about biology and mathematics: how they tie together in interdisciplianry areas. Currently, I am exploring these subjects through conducting a research. Through my career, I hope to explore these subjects more and use the skills in medical field.

More about me

Currently, my research focuses on cell-cell communication of various skin diseases from single-cell RNA sequencing. Using mathematical modeling and computational analysis, I focus on integrating mathematics into biological concepts. Another research interest that I have is utilizing mass spectrometry. Recently, I studied the effects of inhibition of Wnt pathways on the proliferation and metabolome of colorectal cancer cells (SW480 cells) using mass spectrometer. For this project, I used both biology lab and matlab to reach a conclusion that ibuprofen (commonly known as Advil) has a impact on slowing down the proliferation of cancer cells.
